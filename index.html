Why Donate Blood?

Blood donation is a life-saving act that takes just a few minutes of your time but can save up to three lives. Here's why your donation matters:

  • Every 2 seconds, someone in the world needs blood
  • A single car accident victim can require as many as 100 units of blood
  • Blood cannot be manufactured – it can only come from generous donors
  • Less than 10% of eligible population donates blood yearly
  • Blood is needed for surgeries, cancer treatments, chronic illnesses, and traumatic injuries

Your donation today could save someone's life tomorrow. Join our mission to ensure blood supply for those in need.

Why Donate Blood?

Every 2 seconds, someone in the world needs blood. A single donation can save up to 3 lives and help patients undergoing surgery, cancer treatment, childbirth complications, and treating chronic illnesses.

Read More

Donation Process

The donation process is simple, safe, and takes only about 10-15 minutes. After registration and a mini health check, the donation itself is quick and relatively painless, followed by a short rest and refreshments.

Read More

Eligibility Requirements

Most healthy adults aged 18-65 who weigh at least 110 pounds can donate blood. Some factors like recent travel, medications, or certain medical conditions may affect eligibility. Our staff can help determine if you're eligible.

Did You Know?

The average adult has about 10 pints of blood in their body. Roughly 1 pint is given during a donation.

Red blood cells can be stored for up to 42 days, while platelets must be used within just 5 days.

Your body replaces the blood volume (plasma) within 24 hours. Red cells need about four to six weeks for complete replacement.
